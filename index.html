Your travel advisor

Meet Steve Fader

For decades, Steve Fader has perfected a single craft: designing trips that feel nothing like tourism. What began as meticulously planned journeys for his own family has grown into a calling — creating custom, curated trips for travelers all over the world.

His gift is connection. Steve builds personal relationships with guides, hosts, and experience-makers in each place, so his travelers step behind the curtain to the hidden gems most people overlook. And because no two travelers are alike, every itinerary flexes around you — as busy or as restful as you wish.

From Japan to Scandinavia, through Central America and Tanzania, and all the way to the northern slope of Alaska, Steve has spent a lifetime chasing the next special moment to share. He's not in the business of crowded, commercialized tours. He's in the business of memory-making — and he'd love to make yours.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Big Steps Travel?

Big Steps Travel is a custom travel advisory led by Steve Fader. He designs personalized, tailor-made itineraries with vetted local guides and off-the-beaten-path experiences for solo travelers, couples, and friends — anywhere in the world.

What does a travel advisor do that I can't do myself?

Steve brings 40+ years of relationships, insider access, and vetted local guides, plus a plan built entirely around you and personal support before, during, and after your trip. You save hours of research and skip the tourist traps.

How much does it cost to work with Steve?

A design fee based on trip complexity: Curated $350–$500, Tailored $750–$1,500, and Bespoke from $2,500 or 10% of the trip budget. A 50% deposit begins the work and is applied toward your travels.

Why do you charge a planning fee?

The fee covers Steve's time, research, and expertise — about 78% of advisors now charge one. It also means your itinerary is designed for you, not built to push a pre-set package.

Where can Steve plan trips?

Worldwide. Highlights include Japan, Northern & Eastern Europe, Scandinavia, Tanzania, Costa Rica, Italy, and Alaska — but he designs custom trips to virtually any destination.

Who do you plan trips for?

Solo travelers, couples, and friends — at any pace, from packed with discovery to slow and restful.

How far in advance should I reach out?

As early as you can. For complex or peak-season trips, 3–6 months or more is ideal, though Steve will always do his best with shorter timelines.
